01 — What is Factor Investing?

Factor investing is a rules-based investment approach that selects stocks based on proven characteristics — called "factors" — that have historically delivered better returns than the broad market over long periods. Unlike traditional index investing or active fund investing, factor investing uses systematic, pre-defined rules. No emotions. No predictions. Just disciplined execution.

Index says: Buy everything by market cap.
Active says: Pick the best stocks.
Factor says: Buy proven characteristics.

06 — The Six Rules of Autopilot Investing

01
Rebalance once every January. Check weights, sell what's grown above target, buy what's fallen below. 30 minutes. Done.
02
Deploy new money to underweight sleeves. New capital goes to whichever ETF is most below its target weight. Automatic buy-low behaviour.
03
Never question the 25% Gold allocation. Gold drags in strong bull years. That is expected. Every single crisis — 2008, 2011, 2020, 2022, 2025 — Gold rose while equities fell.
04
Never make changes mid-year. Markets fall. A factor lags. The news is terrible. Your instinct says act. Do not act. Check in January. Do nothing now.
05
Factor lag is part of the strategy. Value lagged 5 years (2013–2019). Then returned +58% in 2023. The system captures recovery — but only if you hold through the lag.
06
Stop optimising — start executing. The portfolio is already optimised on 18 years of data. Resist adding a 6th factor or changing allocations based on recent performance.

07 — Tax Considerations

ETF gains held for more than 1 year are taxed as LTCG at 12.5% above ₹1.25L per financial year. Short term gains (under 1 year) are taxed at 20%. Annual rebalancing may trigger LTCG — factor this into your planning. Consult your CA for personalised tax advice.

01 — What Is This System?

The S8 Dynamic System is a rule-based, regime-driven allocation engine. It does not predict markets. It reads two observable signals every month and repositions into one of four pre-defined allocations — nothing more.

Three things create the edge:
1. Drawdown control — max −16.3% vs −58% for Nifty in 2008–09
2. Factor rotation — right ETFs at the right time
3. Gold as a crisis engine — 35% in Bear+Fear
What this system is NOT:
✗ Not a market timing tool — won't buy bottoms or sell tops
✗ Not a trading system — avg switch once every 3–4 months
✗ Not passive — monthly signal checks required without fail
✗ Not infallible — will underperform in strong bull years

05 — Monthly Process (5–10 minutes at month end)

1
Check Signal 1 — On the last trading day, check whether Nifty 50 is above or below its 21-week moving average. Result: BULL or BEAR.
2
Check Signal 2 — On the same day, check India VIX on nseindia.com. Result: CALM (below 20) or FEAR (at/above 20).
3
Identify Regime — Combine both results. E.g. Nifty above MA + VIX below 20 = BULL + CALM. Refer to the regime table above.
4
Regime Changed? — Compare today's regime to last month's. If the same — do nothing. Rebalance only when the regime has changed.
5
Rebalance if Changed — Adjust portfolio to new regime's weights within 2–3 trading days. Execute sells first, then buys.
—
Check frequency: Bull+Calm 57% · Bull+Fear 18% · Bear+Calm 14% · Bear+Fear 11% of all months historically.
